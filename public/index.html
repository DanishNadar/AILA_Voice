Before you begin

AILA Terms of Use and Privacy Notice

You must review and accept these terms before using AILA. AILA is a leadership practice tool for educational and professional development use. Effective date: 2026-03-20.

Purpose of the app

AILA is designed to help users practice leadership conversations, reflect on communication choices, and learn better leadership strategies. It is not a substitute for legal advice, HR advice, mental health care, or emergency support.

What the app sends and does not store

The app itself is designed not to store conversation transcripts in a database. During a session, messages are kept in browser memory so the conversation can continue. Imported scenarios can be stored locally in the browser for convenience, but acceptance of these terms is not remembered between app loads in this build.

How AI processing works

When you send a message or audio clip, AILA sends that request through its serverless API to an external inference provider so the app can generate a response or transcribe speech. In the default production setup for this project, that provider is Groq.

Privacy and provider handling

Groq states that customer data is not retained by default for inference requests, except in limited circumstances such as reliability troubleshooting or abuse investigation. Groq also offers a Zero Data Retention setting that an organization administrator can enable. Your deployment team is responsible for configuring provider settings appropriately.

No training promise from the app

This application does not store your responses to build its own training set. Provider-side handling is governed by the provider's platform settings and policies.

Appropriate use

Do not submit sensitive personal data, regulated health information, payment card information, or confidential information unless your organization has approved that use and configured the provider accordingly.

User responsibility

You are responsible for how you use the app and for reviewing AI-generated content critically. Practice conversations may be helpful, but they can still be incomplete, mistaken, or contextually imperfect.

By continuing, you acknowledge that AILA is a practice and reflection tool and that your organization controls its deployment configuration.
